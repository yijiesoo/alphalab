📊 What We Calculate

Our 5 Main Indicators

1. 12-Month Momentum (50% weight)

Measures long-term price trend over the past 12 months (excluding last 30 days)

Momentum = (Price 1 month ago - Price 12 months ago) / Price 12 months ago

What it tells us: Is the stock in a sustained uptrend or downtrend?

Data source: Historical prices from yfinance (Yahoo Finance)

2. 14-Day RSI - Relative Strength Index (50% weight)

Measures short-term momentum using gains vs losses over 14 days

RSI = 100 - (100 / (1 + RS))
RS = Average Gain / Average Loss (over 14 days)

What it tells us: Is the stock oversold (buying opportunity) or overbought (selling opportunity)?

Data source: Daily price changes from yfinance

3. VIX (Market Fear Index)

Measures overall market fear and volatility

What it tells us: Is the market calm or nervous? High VIX = higher risk.

Data source: Real-time VIX index from yfinance

4. News Sentiment

Analyzes recent news headlines using FinBERT (AI trained on financial text)

What it tells us: Are people saying good or bad things about this stock?

Data source: News headlines from NewsAPI (when available)

5. Confidence Score

Combines all signals to show how certain we are

Confidence = (extremeness × 0.6 + signal_agreement × 0.4) × 100

Extremeness: How extreme is the score? (0-1)
Signal Agreement: How many signals align? (0-1)

What it tells us: Should you trust this signal? (0-95%, capped for humility)

🎯 How We Calculate the Final Score

FINAL SCORE = (Momentum Score × 0.5) + (RSI Score × 0.5)

Range: 0-100
75-100: STRONG BUY
60-75: BUY
40-60: HOLD
25-40: SELL
0-25: STRONG SELL

Why These Weights?

  • 50% Momentum: Shows long-term trend (weeks/months) - most important for buy-and-hold
  • 50% RSI: Shows short-term exhaustion (days/weeks) - helps time entry/exit
✅ Real Data Only: Every number comes from actual market data. No guessing, no placeholders.

🤔 Our Assumptions (What We Assume is True)

Assumption 1: Historical Patterns Repeat
We assume that price patterns observed in the past will continue in the future. This is NOT always true.
Assumption 2: 14 Days is Optimal for RSI
We use 14-day RSI because it's the industry standard. But different stocks may perform better with different periods (7-day, 21-day, etc.).
Assumption 3: Equal Weighting (50/50)
We weight momentum and RSI equally. Some traders prefer 60/40, 70/30, or even 100/0. Our choice is arbitrary.
Assumption 4: VIX Below 20 = Normal
We consider VIX < 20 as normal market conditions. During crises, this may not apply.
Assumption 5: Sentiment Analysis is Accurate
We use FinBERT for sentiment, but AI can misunderstand context, sarcasm, or nuance in financial news.
Assumption 6: Stocks Move Independently
We analyze each stock in isolation. In reality, stocks move together during market crashes or rallies.

⚖️ Known Biases & Limitations

Recency Bias

What it means: We heavily weight recent price movements (14-day RSI). Older patterns (12-month momentum) matter less.
Risk: After a big drop, RSI is oversold (we say "BUY"). But momentum might still be down (we say "SELL"). These contradict each other.
Mitigation: We show both signals. Look at both.

Survivorship Bias

What it means: We only show stocks that still exist. Bankrupt companies are ignored.
Risk: Our signals are better than they seem because we never see failed trades.
Mitigation: Remember: some trades WILL lose money. No indicator is perfect.

Look-Ahead Bias

What it means: We calculate today's signal using today's data. We don't accidentally use tomorrow's data (which would be cheating).
Mitigation: ✅ We actively prevent this in our code. You can check the source.

Trending Market Bias

What it means: Our signals work better when markets are trending. In choppy, sideways markets, they fail more often.
Risk: During consolidation periods, you might get false signals.
Mitigation: We show confidence % to warn you when signals are weak.

Gap Risk

What it means: Stock can gap overnight on bad news. RSI doesn't predict gaps.
Risk: You could enter a trade and see -20% overnight.
Mitigation: Use stop-losses. Never risk money you can't afford to lose.

Sentiment Misclassification

What it means: FinBERT (our AI) can misread headlines. "Stock crashes" might be labeled positive if context is hidden.
Risk: Sentiment scores might be wrong.
Mitigation: We show the raw headline count. Check if sentiment makes sense.

❌ What We DON'T Predict

  • Earnings surprises: We don't know if AAPL will beat earnings next week
  • Product launches: We can't predict a Tesla recall or new iPhone release
  • CEO scandals: We don't follow news about leadership changes in real-time
  • Market crashes: Black Swan events (wars, pandemics) can override all signals
  • Competitive threats: A new competitor entering the market
  • Regulatory changes: New government policies affecting the industry
  • Interest rate changes: Fed policy shifts that affect stock valuations
  • Sector rotation: Money moving from tech to healthcare, etc.
🚨 These factors matter more than our signals sometimes. Always check the news before trading.
